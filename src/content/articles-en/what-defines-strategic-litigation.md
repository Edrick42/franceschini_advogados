Some disputes fit into an operational response. Others require strategic reading from the very first moment. Telling them apart is the first task of any lawyer hired for a high-impact matter.

## When litigation is strategic

A dispute is strategic when its outcome touches the structure of the client's business, assets or institutional reputation. It is not about the isolated amount at stake — it is about systemic consequence.

In these matters, the legal thesis does not emerge from precedent — it is built from the scenario. And the defense cannot be merely reactive.

## The cost of a late response

Treating a strategic matter as routine is expensive. Procedural decisions made early on — venue, initial drafting, evidentiary structure — define the terrain the case will develop on.

By the time the client realizes the matter is strategic, it is often too late to redirect.
